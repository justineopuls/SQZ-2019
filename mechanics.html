ELIMINATION ROUND

This stage will test the written knowledge, skill and speed of the students. It is designed to see how many questions they can correctly answer in a limited amount of time.

  1. Three (3) students per team will take a written examination individually.
  2. Only students registered as a member or alternate for the team will be allowed to participate.
  3. Two (2) hours and thirty (30) minutes will be provided to answer the given exam.
  4. The exam is composed of three (3) parts: Easy, Average, and Difficult. Each part is composed of ten (10) questions for a total of thirty (30) questions.
  5. Correctly answered questions will be credited based on the difficulty. No deductions will be given for incorrect or unanswered questions.

  6. A seating arrangement will be assigned for each participant.
  7. No discussion will be allowed between participants for the duration of the round.
  8. As the exam time expires, all papers will be collected by the proctors.
  9. The score of a team will be the sum of the individual scores of the members of the team.
  10. The twenty (20) teams with the highest scores will advance to the next round.
  11. In case of a tie, the following rules will apply:
    1. The individual scores of each member of the team will be compared.
      1. The team with the higher top individual score will advance to the next round.
      2. If the top individual scores are the same, the next top individual score will be compared. The rule stated above will also be applied.
    2. If the method above does not settle the issue, the teams involved in the tie after applying the previous rule will be asked to answer a tiebreaker question as a group. After giving the question, the teams will be allowed to continually give answers within the allotted time. The team with the first correct answer will advance to the next round.
  12. Scores of this examination will not be released.
  13. The top three (3) individual participants with the highest scores will be given special recognition.

SEMI-FINAL ROUND

This stage will test your knowledge, intelligence, accuracy and endurance. It is designed to see how far you can go.

  1. This stage is the survival of the fittest, where for every checkpoint, the team with the lowest score is eliminated.
  2. The team will work as a group to answer the questions.
  3. The scores of the teams in the Elimination Round will not be carried over to the Semi-Final Round; hence, their scores will be reverted back to zero (0).
  4. This round is comprised of a maximum of fifty (50) questions. Each question must be answered within the time limit. If the team’s answer is correct, they will be awarded points that correspond to the question’s difficulty. No deductions will be given for incorrect or unanswered questions.

Teams who fail to comply will be penalized. The team will be warned on the first offense; the team’s answer will be void on the second offense; and the team will be disqualified on the third offense.
  8. Boards will be provided for the answers. Final answers will be raised after the timer expires. Only the answer written on the board will be considered. If a team is caught changing or still writing their answers after the timer stops, the team’s answer will be void on the first offense, and the team will be disqualified on the second offense.
  9. Questions will only be entertained before the quizmaster reads the next question.
  10. The decisions of the panel of judges will be final.
  11. The 5 remaining teams not eliminated in this round will proceed to the Final Round.

FINAL ROUND

The Final Round will consist purely of problem solving questions specially made to test both the conceptual and technical understanding of the subject matter. It is designed in a way that the questions are difficult and tricky that it will need a group with the most strategy, knowledge, accuracy, teamwork and guts to win.

  1. The Final Round will be the last stage of SquEEEze.
  2. The scores of the teams in the Semi-Final Round will not be carried over to the Final Round; hence, their scores will be reverted back to zero (0).
  3. This round is comprised of thirty (30 questions). Each category will have three (3) difficulties.

  4. Teams will take turns in selecting the question’s category (e.g. Team A will select the category for the first question, Team B will select the category for the second question, and so on). The difficulty for the question will be completely random, however.
  5. Each team will work as a group to answer the questions. It is important to note that all teams are allowed to answer the questions including those that are chosen by other teams.
  6. The quizmaster will read the question twice. Contestants are only allowed to write or to use their calculators after the quizmaster has given the signal “Go” or “Timer starts now”. Teams who fail to comply will be penalized. The team will be warned on the first offense; the team’s answer will be void on the second offense; and the team will be disqualified on the third offense.
  7. Teams will use buzzers to indicate that they are ready to answer. A buzzer test will be conducted before the Final Round. In case of a buzzer failure, flags will be used instead.
  8. Boards will be provided for the answers. Only the answer written on the board will be considered.
  9. The first team to ring their buzzer will be given 5 seconds to declare their answer. If their answer is correct, they will be awarded points that correspond to the question’s difficulty. However, if the answer is incorrect, deduction of two-thirds the amount of points that correspond to the question will be given.
  10. In case of a wrong answer from the first team, other teams will be given the chance to steal. The first team to ring their buzzer for the steal within the remaining time limit will be given 5 seconds to show their answer. The same scoring system applies — answering the question right will grant them equivalent merit points and answering it wrong will result to a demerit of two-thirds the number of points. Only one steal will be offered. Teams may only buzz once the quizmaster has given the signal “Go” or “You may now steal” for the steal.
  11. Questions will only be entertained before the quizmaster reads the next question.
  12. The decisions of the panel of judges will be final.
  13. All teams are required to participate frequently in the Final Round. Teams who have not attempted to answer or steal for five (5) consecutive questions will be given a 6-point deduction. This five-question counter will reset whenever the team receives the deduction and will continue to count up again when necessary.
  14. The round will end after twenty (20) questions are given. Note that this means ten (10) questions from the pool will remain unanswered.
  15. Teams that have accumulated the highest number of points at the end of the Final Round will be considered the winners. The ranking of the teams will also depend on this cumulative score.
  16. In case of a tie for the top three spots, a best-of-five face-off will be used as a tiebreaker. The team who will be able to answer the most number of questions correctly will obtain the higher rank.

CIRCUIT SPRINT

This race will not just test the speed in creating a circuit, but will also test the guts, intuition, and strategy of a team due to the lack of supporting instruments.

  1. Teams are tasked to construct a circuit based on given specifications, to be shown on the event itself, as fast as they can.
  2. Tried and tested materials will be given for each team to work on. Spares will be provided in case of accidents. Note that these are limited since it is expected that careful handling is part of the challenge.
  3. Data sheets, schematics and all of the required values will be given, but it is not assured that everything is present in the given materials. It is assured though that what is given is enough to meet the requirements.
  4. There is no multimeter or any sort of measuring device given. There is only a voltmeter present in the event. Use of the voltmeter is on a per request basis. It is expected that a team will not use the voltmeter for more than one (1) minute at a time.
  5. The circuit to be made will consist of multiple subcircuits which may or may not need each other to operate correctly. Teams may opt to have each of their subcircuits checked up to 3 times without deductions, succeeding checks will incur 15-minute penalties.
  6. The schematics of some subcircuits will be given.
  7. For other subcircuit/s, only a description will be given. It is expected that the team can design their own subcircuit/s. In case a team is unable to make their own design, they may choose to accept a 45-minute penalty in exchange for the design of each subcircuit.
  8. Teams are given two (2) hours to make the circuit function. Teams will be given 3 chances to have their circuits checked without deductions, succeeding checks will incur 15-minute penalties.
  9. The first team to make the whole circuit work will be declared the Champions of Circuit Sprint 2018 and will be given special prizes. The next two succeeding teams will also be recognized.
  10. If less than 3 teams managed to finish the circuit within the time limit, the rankings will be determined by how many subcircuits are completed in the least amount of time.

FORMAT

The paper to be submitted must be of the following format:

  1. Introduction
    1. Background of the Study
      1. Shows the motivation and purpose of the project
    2. Proposal Flow and Organization
      1. Describes the content of each part of the paper
  2. Problem Statement
    • Where the project will revolve to
    • The main question to be answered in the thesis
    • May include sub-questions to support the main question
  3. Review of Related Work
    • Includes past researches that can support the thesis
  4. Methodology
    • A detailed step-by-step process to follow in implementing thesis and how the results and data will be gathered

SCREENING

This stage will examine the projects of the teams. It is designed to see the main idea and the motivation behind the project.

  1. The names included in the paper should only be the students registered as members of the team.
  2. A panel of judges will score the participants’ thesis proposals. The decisions of the panel of judges will be final.
  3. Criteria for judging:
    Review of Related Work 25%
    Problem Statement 15%
    Feasibility 20%
    Preliminary Work 20%
    Motivation 10%
    Overall Structure 10%

TECH DECON

This part will let the teams present their projects to other teams and audiences. It is designed to see how will the teams present their ideas to others and to give them the chance to expand the reach of their project.

  1. Each team will be given two (2) minutes to set up their presentation. After the preparation time, the team will be given five (5) minutes to present their project in front of other teams and audiences. They are allowed to use any visual aids such as powerpoints, demos, or prototypes of their project. Note: The time will start when the team starts speaking.
  2. After each presentation, there will be a Question and Answer (Q&A) portion. Only the judges are allowed to give questions. A maximum of 5 questions may be given by the judges. Answers of the participants must not exceed 1 minute per question. In case the judges ask for a follow-up question, the participants will only be given 30 seconds to answer.
  3. Corresponding deductions to the final grade will be given if the team exceeds the given time for the following:
    1. 2.5% per minute of preparation time unless there are technical difficulties
    2. 5% presentation time
    3. 2% Q&A
  4. A panel of judges will be present to grade the participants. The decisions of the panel of judges will be final.
  5. Criteria for Judging:
    • Content
      • Problem Statement and Review of Related Work 20%
      • Feasibility 15%
    • Presentation
      • Organization 30%
      • Visual aids 5%
    • Q&A 30%
    • Creativity (bonus) 5%
